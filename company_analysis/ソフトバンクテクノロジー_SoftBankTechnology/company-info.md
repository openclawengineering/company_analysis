## 3. Company History & Milestones

### Founding and Early Years
- **1990 (October 16):** Founded as ソフトバンク技研株式会社 (SoftBank Giken Corporation)
- **1991 (July):** Established ソフトバンクネットワークセンター株式会社
- **1997 (August):** Merged SoftBank Giken and SoftBank Network Center to form ヱスビーネットワークス株式会社
- **1998 (August):** Integrated SoftBank Corporation's Information Systems Department
- **1999 (January):** Changed company name to ソフトバンク・テクノロジー株式会社 (SoftBank Technology Corp.)
- **1999 (July):** Registered stock with Japan Securities Dealers Association (JASDAQ)

### Growth and Expansion
- **2001 (March):** Made E-Cosmo Corporation (now M-SOLUTIONS) a subsidiary
- **2002 (May):** Established headquarters in Iidabashi, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo
- **2003 (July):** Opened Ochanomizu office
- **2004 (December 21):** Listed on Tokyo Stock Exchange Second Section
- **2006 (March):** Promoted to Tokyo Stock Exchange First Section

### Consolidation and Restructuring
- **2007 (September):** Absorbed AI Bridge Co., Ltd.
- **2008 (March):** Absorbed SBT Consulting Corporation and SoftBank Mobile Technology Corporation
- **2008 (October):** Opened Nagoya office
- **2010 (April):** Absorbed E-Commerce Technology Corporation
- **2010 (August):** Consolidated Ochanomizu office into Iidabashi office
- **2012 (June):** Opened Shiodome office

### Modern Era
- **2014 (February):** Moved headquarters to Shinjuku Eastside Square
- **2014 (April):** Acquired Cybertrust Corporation as subsidiary
- **2014 (July):** Acquired MIRACLE LINUX for 600 million yen
- **2017 (October):** Merged MIRACLE LINUX and Cybertrust, renamed to Cybertrust Corporation
- **2018 (April):** SoftBank Corporation became direct parent company
- **2019 (October 1):** Changed company name to SBテクノロジー株式会社 (SB Technology Corp.)
- **2020 (July):** Made Den-en Corporation a subsidiary

### Going Private
- **2024 (June):** SoftBank Corporation acquired 88.14% through tender offer
- **2024 (September 6):** Delisted from Tokyo Stock Exchange Prime Market, became wholly-owned subsidiary of SoftBank Corporation
- **2026 (April 1):** Scheduled to be merged into SoftBank Corporation

## 9. Summary

### Company Overview
SBテクノロジー株式会社 (SB Technology Corp., formerly SoftBank Technology Corp.) is a specialized system integrator and IT services company that has operated as a consolidated subsidiary of the SoftBank Group. Founded in 1990, the company has grown to become one of Japan's leading Microsoft solution providers with 1,447 employees and annual consolidated revenue of approximately 65.7 billion yen (FY2024).

### Core Competencies
The company's primary strengths lie in:
1. **Microsoft Technologies:** As a Gold Certified Partner and 10-time consecutive Microsoft Japan Partner of the Year winner, SBT has unparalleled expertise in Microsoft 365, Azure, and Dynamics 365
2. **Cybersecurity:** Managed Security Services (MSS) form a core pillar of their business, with capabilities in zero-trust implementation and incident response
3. **Digital Transformation:** Proven track record in cloud migration, work style reform, and business process automation

### Business Model
SBT operates as a full-service IT partner offering:
- System integration and consulting
- Proprietary add-on services (clouXion brand)
- Managed services with 24/7 support
- Custom development for enterprise clients

### Client Base
The company serves a diverse range of clients across all major industries in Japan, including:
- Government agencies and local municipalities
- Universities and educational institutions
- Major corporations in construction, manufacturing, retail, and services
- Financial institutions
- Healthcare organizations

### Corporate Status
As of September 2024, SBT became a wholly-owned subsidiary of SoftBank Corporation after being delisted from the Tokyo Stock Exchange. The company is scheduled to be fully merged into SoftBank Corporation on April 1, 2026, marking the end of its 34-year journey as an independent entity.

### Strategic Value
SB Technology represents SoftBank's in-house enterprise IT capabilities, particularly in Microsoft technologies and cybersecurity. The planned merger into SoftBank Corporation signals a strategic consolidation to strengthen the parent company's enterprise services offerings and create a more unified service delivery model.

### Future Outlook
Following the planned 2026 merger, SBT's expertise and capabilities will continue within SoftBank Corporation's organizational structure, likely enhancing SoftBank's ability to deliver comprehensive enterprise IT services to its business customers.
