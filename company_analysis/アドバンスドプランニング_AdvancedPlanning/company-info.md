# Company Analysis Report

## アドバンスドプランニング株式会社 (Advanced Planning Corporation)

---

## Executive Summary

**Advanced Planning Corporation** is a specialized Japanese software development company based in Yokohama, focusing exclusively on control systems and embedded software development. Founded in 1994, the company has established itself as a niche player in a less-than-10% segment of the IT industry, differentiating itself by avoiding mainstream business software development in favor of specialized control systems.

## 3. Business Activities & Services

### Core Business Philosophy
The company specializes **100% in contract development**, with approximately **50% dedicated to embedded software development**. They deliberately avoid web-based business software development, focusing instead on control systems—a niche representing less than 10% of the IT industry.

### Primary Business Segments

#### 1. Control System Development (制御系コンピュータシステム)
- Vehicle control-related systems
- Industrial control systems
- Embedded software for specialized hardware

#### 2. Network System Development (ネットワークシステム)
- 920MHz band wireless communication systems
- LTE-based wireless transmission control
- GPS location-based systems
- Traditional wired network monitoring systems

#### 3. Video & Image System Development (映像・画像システム)
- High-definition video conferencing systems
- Multi-location simultaneous video monitoring systems
- Set-Top Box (STB) development
- Advanced video/audio compression technology applications

#### 4. Medical System Development (医療系システム)
- Bedside monitors for hospital rooms
- Operating room monitoring systems
- Medical device software development

## 5. Company Philosophy & Culture

### Core Motto
**"大よりも強" (Stronger rather than Bigger)**

The company aims to be a "strong company" rather than a "large company," emphasizing resilience and capability over scale.

### Mission Statement
> "We always prioritize 'peace of mind and safety' (安心・安全) for users, providing reliable and trustworthy software."

### Quality Policy Principles
1. Accurately capture customer requirements and respond with sincerity
2. Provide high-quality, high-value-added products
3. Each employee takes responsibility and initiative for quality improvement
4. **No compromises**
5. **Always keep promises to customers (delivery deadline compliance)**
6. Focus on human resource development and specialized knowledge enhancement
7. Continuously plan and execute quality activities
8. Verify activities and review operations for continuous improvement
9. Comply with laws and regulations

### Work Culture & Environment

#### Unique Characteristics
- **In-house development model**: Unlike typical IT companies, employees work primarily at their own office in Shin-Yokohama rather than being dispatched to client sites
- **Stylish office design**: Entrance plays jazz and bossa nova music; café-like atmosphere with coffee available
- **Work-life balance emphasis**:
  - Encourages taking consecutive holidays during Golden Week and summer break
  - Typical holidays: ~9 consecutive days during peak vacation periods
  - Average annual overtime: ~240 hours (fully compensated)

#### Philosophy on AI & Future-Proofing
The company positions itself in segments "AI cannot replace"—specialized control software that requires deep hardware integration and cannot be easily automated.

## 9. SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- Deep specialization in control systems (less than 10% of IT market)
- In-house development model (rare in Japanese IT industry)
- Strong certifications (ISO 9001, ISO 27001)
- 32 years of accumulated expertise
- Stable growth trajectory
- Strong work-life balance culture

### Weaknesses
- Small company size (38 employees)
- Limited diversification
- Niche focus may limit market expansion

### Opportunities
- Growing demand for embedded systems in IoT era
- Medical device software market expansion

- AI-resistant technical positioning

### Threats
- Competition from larger system integrators
- Technology evolution requiring continuous adaptation
- Talent acquisition in specialized field

---

## 10. Conclusion

Advanced Planning Corporation represents a distinctive approach in the Japanese IT industry—a company that deliberately chose specialization over diversification, depth over breadth. Their focus on control systems and embedded software places them in a defensible market position, particularly as they emphasize work in areas that AI cannot easily replicate.

The company's philosophy of being "strong rather than big," combined with their in-house development model, creates an unusual but appealing environment in an industry often characterized by client dispatch and work-life imbalance. Their consistent growth over 32 years, multiple office expansions, and strong certification portfolio suggest a sustainable business model built on technical expertise and customer trust.
