## 3. Company History & Milestones

### Foundation and Early Years (1983-1999)
- **February 1983:** Established as 日商岩井コンピュータシステムズ㈱ in Akasaka, Minato-ku, Tokyo
- **June 1987:** Renamed to 日商岩井インフォコムシステムズ㈱
- **April 1999:** Renamed to 日商岩井インフォコム㈱; Established Infocom Services Corporation (now Infocom Technologies)

### Restructuring and Growth (2000-2010)
- **April 2000:** Renamed to インフォコム㈱ (Infocom Corporation)
- **March 2001:** Moved headquarters to Kanda Surugadai, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo; Merged with Teijin System Technology Corporation
- **March 2002:** Registered as over-the-counter stock with Japan Securities Dealers Association
- **October 2003:** Established Infobec Corporation (renamed to GRANDIT Corporation in 2012)
- **December 2004:** Listed on JASDAQ Securities Exchange
- **March 2006:** Acquired data center in Yokohama
- **November 2006:** Started e-book distribution service in network business
- **July 2008:** Moved headquarters to Jingumae, Shibuya-ku, Tokyo
- **April 2010:** Listed on Osaka Securities Exchange JASDAQ Market

### Expansion and Reorganization (2013-2024)
- **April 2013:** Acquired radiation department system business from AJS Corporation
- **July 2013:** Listed on Tokyo Stock Exchange JASDAQ Market (Standard); Established Iwanuma "Minna no Ie" as East Japan Earthquake recovery support base
- **August 2017:** Sold Yokohama data center
- **November 2018:** Transferred to Tokyo Stock Exchange First Section
- **October 2021:** Made Medical Create Corporation a group company; Moved headquarters to Akasaka, Minato-ku, Tokyo
- **April 2022:** Transferred to Tokyo Stock Exchange Prime Market
- **April 2023:** Made J-MAC System Corporation a group company

### Major Ownership Changes (2024-2025)
- **June 2024:** Blackstone announced TOB (Tender Offer) at ¥6,060 per share (¥275.7 billion total) - Blackstone's largest acquisition in Japan
- **October 16, 2024:** Delisted from Tokyo Stock Exchange Prime Market after 20+ years as a listed company
- **April 2025:** Merged with GRANDIT Corporation
- **July 2025:** Separated network business from group
- **July 2025:** Became a group company of Nippon Steel Solutions Corporation (日鉄ソリューションズ㈱)

## 9. Summary

### Company Overview
Infocom Corporation (株式会社インフォコム) is a comprehensive Japanese IT solutions provider with over 40 years of history since its establishment in 1983. Originally founded as a subsidiary of Nichimen Corporation (now Sojitz), the company has evolved into a multifaceted technology services provider with particular strength in healthcare IT solutions.

### Key Strengths
1. **Healthcare IT Leadership:** Market-leading position in clinical information management (Medi-Bank) and pharmaceutical information systems (DICS), serving over 2,500 medical institutions across Japan
2. **Diversified Business Model:** Strong presence in both B2B enterprise services (ERP, system integration) and consumer digital content (electronic comics through Mecha-Comi)
3. **Technical Excellence:** Proven track record in developing and operating large-scale systems with expertise in AI, cloud computing, and mobile technologies
4. **Customer Base:** Long-standing relationships with major corporations, medical institutions, pharmaceutical companies, and public sector organizations

### Recent Transformation (2024-2025)
The company underwent significant ownership changes, being acquired by Blackstone (the US private equity firm's largest Japanese acquisition) in October 2024, delisting from the Tokyo Stock Exchange after 20 years. Subsequently, in July 2025, it became a subsidiary of Nippon Steel Solutions Corporation (NSSOL), marking a new chapter in its corporate evolution. The network business (including the profitable Mecha-Comi electronic comic service) was separated from the group in July 2025.

### Business Segments
1. **Healthcare Services:** Medical IT systems, radiology information systems, pharmaceutical information management
2. **Enterprise Services:** ERP systems (GRANDIT), system integration, cloud services
3. **IT Solutions:** Custom development, system operation, mobile solutions
4. **Business Development:** Startup support, technology R&D

### Financial Performance
The company demonstrated strong financial performance with FY2024 consolidated revenue of ¥844.53 billion (20.1% YoY growth) and operating profit of ¥9.784 billion, with the network business (electronic comics) contributing significantly to profitability.

### Strategic Direction
Under new ownership within the NSSOL group, Infocom is positioned to:
- Leverage synergies with Nippon Steel Solutions' extensive client base and technical capabilities
- Focus on core IT services and healthcare solutions
- Continue innovation in AI-powered diagnostic and business solutions
- Maintain commitment to customer success and social contribution through technology

### Corporate Culture
Infocom emphasizes a customer-centric, innovation-driven culture with strong values around teamwork, continuous learning, and ethical business practices. The company views employees as "人財" (human assets) and invests heavily in professional development and work-life balance initiatives.

### Future Outlook
As a newly integrated member of the NSSOL group, Infocom is positioned to enhance its market position in healthcare IT and enterprise solutions while benefiting from the stability and resources of its new parent company. The separation of the network business allows renewed focus on core B2B IT services, positioning the company for sustainable growth in Japan's evolving digital landscape.
