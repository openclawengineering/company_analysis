## Executive Summary

富士通中部システムズ (Fujitsu Chubu Systems Limited, abbreviated FJCL) was a **defunct** Japanese system integrator and wholly-owned subsidiary of Fujitsu Limited. The company served the Tokai/Chubu region of Japan before being merged into **富士通システムズ・ウエスト (Fujitsu Systems West)** in April 2012 as part of Fujitsu's regional subsidiary consolidation.

## 2012 Merger & Successor

### Consolidation Structure
Fujitsu Chubu Systems was merged into **富士通システムズ・ウエスト (Fujitsu Systems West)** along with 5 other regional SE subsidiaries:

1. 富士通関西システムズ (Fujitsu Kansai Systems) - **Surviving company**
2. 富士通中部システムズ (Fujitsu Chubu Systems)
3. 富士通中国システムズ (Fujitsu Chugoku Systems)
4. 富士通岡山システムエンジニアリング (Fujitsu Okayama Systems Engineering)
5. 富士通四国システムズ (Fujitsu Shikoku Systems)
6. 富士通西日本アプリケーション (Fujitsu West Japan Application)

### Successor Information
- **Company Name:** 富士通システムズ・ウエスト株式会社 (Fujitsu Systems West Limited)
- **Headquarters:** Osaka, Japan
- **Purpose:** Unified system integration services for Western Japan region

## Market Position & Competitive Analysis

### Strengths (Historical)
- Strong regional presence in the Chubu/Tokai industrial corridor
- Deep expertise in manufacturing industry IT (Toyota region)
- 100% Fujitsu backing providing access to enterprise resources
- Certified quality and environmental management systems
- Local presence with multiple offices across the region

### Business Context
The Chubu region is home to Japan's manufacturing heartland, including:
- Toyota Motor Corporation and its supplier network
- Aerospace and automotive industries
- Heavy manufacturing sectors

This positioned FJCL as a critical IT partner for manufacturing digitalization.
