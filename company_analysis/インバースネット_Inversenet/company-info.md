## 2. Company History

### Founding & Early Years (1940s-1950s)
- **February 1947**: Founded as "光電気工業" (Hikari Electric Industry) in Tamon-cho, Minato-ku, Tokyo
- **February 1949**: Became a designated vendor for Nippon Telegraph and Telephone Corporation (日本電信電話公社), beginning communication equipment retrieval and dismantling operations
- **December 1951**: Established "光金属工業株式会社" (Hikari Metal Industry Co., Ltd.) in Higashi-Nakanobu, Shinagawa-ku with capital of ¥500,000

### Expansion Era (1960s-1990s)
- **December 1962**: Relocated headquarters to Minami-Yukigaya, Ota-ku; renamed to "新光金属工業株式会社" (Shinko Metal Industry Co., Ltd.)
- **March 1968**: Established dismantling plant in Toriyama-cho, Tochigi Prefecture
- **January 1983**: Started business with leasing companies; began selling used equipment
- **January 1985**: Opened Koto sales office for used equipment sales expansion
- **September 1985**: Opened Osaka sales office to expand leasing industry relationships
- **1989-1999**: Multiple office expansions across Japan (Chiba, Nagoya, Fukuoka, Kawasaki, Yokohama)

### Modern Era (2000s-Present)
- **March 2000**: Changed company name to **インバースネット株式会社** (Inversenet Inc.)
- **October 2001**: Established Shop Inverse Corporation
- **April 2003**: Merged Shop Inverse Corporation
- **July 2003**: **Business partnership with Yamada Denki (株式会社ヤマダ電機)**
- **December 2003**: **Capital partnership with Yamada Denki**
- **August 2004**: Relocated headquarters to Yokohama City, Kanagawa Prefecture
- **August 2005**: Established Repair Center in Nasu-Karasuyama City, Tochigi Prefecture
- **July 2006**: Obtained ISMS (ISO27001) certification
- **September 2008**: Expanded ISMS (ISO27001) certification to all offices
- **July 2013**: **Acquired PC business from KOUZIRO Corporation (株式会社KOUZIRO)**
- **2010-2023**: Expanded retail stores across Japan (Akihabara, Nihonbashi, Nagoya, Sapporo, Takadanobaba)

## Summary

インバースネット株式会社 (Inversenet Inc.) is a long-established Japanese IT company with over **75 years of history** (founded 1947). The company has evolved from a telecommunications equipment recycler into a comprehensive IT solutions provider with five core business pillars: BTO PC manufacturing (FRONTIER brand), recycling, reuse (buyback/sales), repair, and rental services.

The company maintains a strategic partnership with **Yamada Denki**, Japan's largest electronics retailer, and has built a nationwide network of offices, recycling centers, and retail stores. With strong environmental credentials (ISO27001 certified, licensed waste handler) and a focus on sustainable IT lifecycle management, Inversenet exemplifies the Japanese approach to responsible technology stewardship—connecting past values to future innovations through their philosophy of "Value Forms a Circle."
