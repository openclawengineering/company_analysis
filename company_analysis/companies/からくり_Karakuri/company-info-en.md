## Philosophy

### Corporate Philosophy

The name "Karakuri" comes from the famous Edo-period mechanical automaton (Karakuri doll), representing wisdom and ingenuity passed down through generations. This name reflects the company's mission to inherit Japan's traditional craftsmanship and respond flexibly to technological innovation.

### President's Message

What comes to mind when you hear the company name "Karakuri"?

Karakuri refers to traditional Japanese mechanical dolls and devices, but originally it was a word that referred to machines in general.

Karakuri were devices packed with cutting-edge technology at the time, their movements amazed and moved people, and above all, their various mechanisms contained humor that captivated people.

The mechanical treatise "Karakuri Zui" (Illustrated Compendium of Mechanical Devices), attributed to Hosokawa Hanzo in 1796 (Kansei 8), states, "Though it is truly no different from child's play, depending on the viewer's judgment, it may serve as an aid to the awakening and creation of the mind (it is not much different from making toys, but for some, it may serve as an aid to the invention of various mechanisms)."

I cannot help but believe that this spirit underpinned later technological innovation in Japan.

Currently, computers handle almost all of the mechanisms that operate machines.

As a Japanese IT company, we founded our business with the aim of developing systems that can perform the kind of sophisticated and ingenious work reminiscent of karakuri.
