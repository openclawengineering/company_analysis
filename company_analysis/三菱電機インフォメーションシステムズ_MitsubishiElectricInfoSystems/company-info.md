## 3. Company History & Milestones

### Original Company (MIND) History
- **2001:** 三菱電機インフォメーションシステムズ株式会社 (MIND) was established through the spin-off of Mitsubishi Electric's Information Systems Business Headquarters
- **2001-2025:** Operated as a subsidiary focusing on system integration, software development, and IT services

### Mitsubishi Electric IT Division History (Pre-MIND)
- **1960:** Mitsubishi Electric's first digital computer "MELCOM1101" completed
- **1961:** First domestic accounting machine "MAM21" released
- **1968:** Office computer "MELCOM 81" released (origin of "off-con" term)
- **1971:** Domestic highest-performance minicomputer "MELCOM-70" released
- **1974:** Large-scale general-purpose computer "MELCOM-COSMO 700" released (joint development with OKI)
- **1981:** Japan's first 16-bit business PC "MULTI16" released
- **1982:** First 32-bit office computer "MELCOM 80 Office Land Series" released
- **1986:** World's first PROLOG AI workstation "MELCOM PSI" released
- **1988:** Group digital integrated communication network "MIND" service started
- **1989:** Mitsubishi Electric Information Network Corporation established
- **1989:** Database processing accelerator "GREO" RDB processor released (up to 50x faster)
- **1997:** Information security solution "MistyGuard" series first product "CRYPTOFILE" released
- **2000:** DIAPRISM achieved world record in Datamation benchmark test
- **2001:** Information Systems Division reorganized; MIND and MIT (Mitsubishi Electric Information Technology) established
- **2010:** MIND's first SaaS product "MELFOS on Demand" FAX OCR service released
- **2014:** Mitsubishi Electric Information Network and MIT merged to form Mitsubishi Electric Information Network Corporation
- **2017:** AI-based video analysis solution "kizkia" released using Maisart technology
- **2020:** MIND, Mitsubishi Electric Information Network, and Mitsubishi Electric Business Systems business structure reorganized; MBS renamed to Mitsubishi Electric IT Solutions
- **2022:** MIND Cyber Fusion Center established, CSIRT operation support service launched
- **2023:** Cloud Centric Corporation established as joint venture with Skyarch Networks

### Major Merger (2025)
- **April 2025:** MIND merged with 三菱電機インフォメーションネットワーク株式会社 (Mitsubishi Electric Information Network Corporation) and 三菱電機ITソリューションズ株式会社 (Mitsubishi Electric IT Solutions Corporation) to form **三菱電機デジタルイノベーション株式会社 (Mitsubishi Electric Digital Innovation Corporation)**

## 9. Summary

三菱電機インフォメーションシステムズ株式会社 (MIND - Mitsubishi Electric Information Systems Corporation) was a major IT subsidiary of Mitsubishi Electric, specializing in system integration, software development, and IT services. Established in 2001 through the spin-off of Mitsubishi Electric's information systems division, MIND built upon over 60 years of Mitsubishi Electric's computing heritage dating back to the MELCOM1101 in 1960.

In April 2025, MIND merged with two other Mitsubishi Electric IT subsidiaries—Mitsubishi Electric Information Network Corporation and Mitsubishi Electric IT Solutions Corporation—to form **Mitsubishi Electric Digital Innovation Corporation** (三菱電機デジタルイノベーション株式会社). This strategic consolidation created a powerhouse with approximately 6,000 employees and 1.25 billion JPY in capital, positioning the company as a "Circular Digital Engineering Company" leveraging the Serendie® digital platform.

The company offers comprehensive solutions across three main business domains: Manufacturing DX Solutions, Infrastructure & Security Solutions, and Solution Services. Key products include the AI-powered video analysis solution kizkia, the Cyber Fusion Center for advanced security operations, cloud-based pharmacy systems (AnyCOMPASS), and various enterprise solutions for HR, manufacturing, and distribution industries.

With a nationwide network of offices from Hokkaido to Kyushu, and backed by the technological heritage and resources of the Mitsubishi Electric Group, the company continues to serve diverse industries including manufacturing, healthcare, finance, public sector, and infrastructure, contributing to both customer growth and the resolution of social challenges through digital innovation.
